# 30-Day LinkedIn Content Calendar For Real Estate Zoho CRM

Post 5 times per week. Keep posts practical. End with a simple point of view, not a desperate CTA.

## Week 1: Problem Awareness

### Post 1: 5 Signs Your Real Estate Zoho CRM Is Leaking Revenue

Hook:

> Your Zoho CRM can look organized and still leak property revenue every day.

Points:

- New inquiries sit without agent assignment.
- Follow-ups depend on memory or WhatsApp reminders.
- Pipeline stages do not match real buyer, seller, rental, booking, or closing conversations.
- Site visits, bookings, commissions, or payments happen outside the CRM.
- Reports look useful but the data is not trusted.

Close:

> Before rebuilding Zoho, audit where real estate revenue is leaking.

### Post 2: The Difference Between Zoho Customization And Zoho Architecture

Hook:

> Customizing Zoho is easy. Architecting Zoho around a real estate business is the hard part.

Points:

- Customization adds fields and workflows.
- Architecture maps how inquiries, agents, listings, tasks, site visits, offers, bookings, payments, commissions, and reports move.
- Bad architecture creates automation debt.
- Good architecture makes the CRM easier to trust.

Close:

> A real estate CRM should match the sales process, not just collect fields.

### Post 3: Why Zoho Workflows Break After 6 Months

Hook:

> Most Zoho workflow problems are not technical. They are process problems that became technical problems.

Points:

- Lead sources changed.
- Agent assignment rules changed.
- Required fields were never updated.
- Users created workarounds.
- Old automations still run.
- No one owns cleanup.

Close:

> Workflow maintenance should be part of real estate CRM operations, not emergency repair.

### Post 4: What I Check First In A Messy Real Estate Zoho CRM

Hook:

> When I open a messy real estate Zoho CRM, I do not start with automations.

Points:

- Lead sources
- Agent assignment
- Property interest and budget fields
- Pipeline stages
- Required fields
- Duplicate contacts and properties
- Follow-up tasks
- Reports the team actually uses

Close:

> Automations only help when the underlying real estate process is clean.

### Post 5: Cheap CRM Setup Becomes Expensive Later

Hook:

> The cheapest real estate CRM setup is often the most expensive one six months later.

Points:

- No process map
- Too many custom fields
- Weak naming conventions
- Workflows built without testing
- No dashboard plan
- No handover documentation

Close:

> A CRM is not finished when it starts working. It is finished when the team can trust it.

## Week 2: Education

### Post 6: Real Estate Lead Assignment Rules

Hook:

> If agent assignment is unclear, your real estate CRM is already leaking.

Points:

- Round-robin is not always enough.
- Territory, location, property type, budget, source, and lead intent matter.
- Every new inquiry should have an owner, task, and next step.
- Unassigned leads should trigger alerts.

Close:

> The first 5 minutes after a property inquiry arrives matter more than most dashboards.

### Post 7: Zoho CRM + Books Mistakes In Real Estate

Hook:

> Zoho CRM and Zoho Books can save hours in real estate, or create a second mess.

Points:

- Deal stages are not aligned with booking, payment, or commission status.
- Properties, units, packages, fees, or commission items are inconsistent.
- Tax, commission, and payment rules are unclear.
- Sales teams do not know where booking or invoicing starts.
- Payment or commission data never returns to CRM reporting.

Close:

> Lead-to-closing needs one clean path, not scattered handoffs.

### Post 8: When To Use Zoho Creator In Real Estate

Hook:

> Not every real estate CRM problem should be solved inside Zoho CRM.

Points:

- Use CRM for leads, contacts, agents, deals, and customer records.
- Use Creator for property inventory, site visit apps, document workflows, maintenance requests, or complex internal operations.
- Avoid forcing complex operational apps into CRM modules.
- Sync only the data CRM needs.

Close:

> The best Zoho systems use the right app for the right job.

### Post 9: Deluge Automation Mistakes

Hook:

> A Deluge script can fix a real estate process. It can also hide a broken one.

Points:

- No error handling
- Hard-coded IDs
- No logging
- No naming convention
- No test records
- No documentation

Close:

> Good Deluge work is boring, traceable, and easy to maintain.

### Post 10: AI Needs Clean CRM Data

Hook:

> AI will not save a messy real estate CRM. It will summarize the mess faster.

Points:

- Bad lead sources create bad scoring.
- Missing budget, location, property type, and timeline fields weaken recommendations.
- Duplicate contacts confuse summaries.
- Poor stage design ruins forecasting.

Close:

> Before adding AI to Zoho, clean the CRM logic first.
