# 30-Day Action Plan For Real Estate Zoho CRM Audits

Goal:

> Get 3 paid Zoho CRM Revenue Leak Audits from real estate businesses and convert at least 1 into an implementation sprint.

## Week 1: Package The Offer

### Day 1

- Read the audit offer.
- Choose your starting price: $300, $500, or $750.
- Recommended: $500.
- Choose one target market: real estate businesses using Zoho.

### Day 2

- Create a simple one-page PDF or website page from `01-Zoho-CRM-Revenue-Leak-Audit.md`.
- Add your name, contact, and booking link.
- Make the page speak directly to brokerages, real estate agencies, developers, or property managers.

### Day 3

- Write 3 case studies using `04-Case-Study-Template.md`.
- Use anonymized projects if needed.
- Reframe proof around lead routing, agent follow-up, pipeline cleanup, portal/form integrations, WhatsApp/SMS workflows, bookings, payments, commissions, and reporting.

### Day 4

- Update LinkedIn headline.
- Suggested headline:

> Zoho CRM & Creator Developer For Real Estate | Lead-to-Closing Automation, Deluge, APIs & AI Workflows

### Day 5

- Update LinkedIn About section.
- Add the audit offer to Featured.
- Add 1 real estate-focused case study to Featured.

### Day 6

- Update Upwork title and first 250 characters.
- Add the audit as a Project Catalog offer.
- Use real estate keywords in the title, description, and tags.

### Day 7

- Prepare a list of 50 contacts:
  - past real estate clients
  - old Zoho leads in real estate
  - real estate agencies
  - property developers
  - property managers
  - real estate marketing agencies
  - property-tech consultants
  - Zoho consultants serving real estate clients
  - founders or operators using Zoho
  - sales/ops consultants serving real estate teams

## Week 2: Start Conversations

Daily targets:

- Send 5 direct messages.
- Post once on LinkedIn.
- Comment on 5 relevant real estate, Zoho, CRM, sales ops, or property-tech posts.
- Add 5 new prospects to your list.

End-of-week target:

- 25 messages sent
- 5 posts published
- 5-10 replies
- 1-2 audit calls booked

## Week 3: Sell The Audit

Daily targets:

- Send 5 more messages.
- Follow up with old replies.
- Offer the audit clearly.
- Keep posting practical Zoho content for real estate businesses.

Call structure:

1. Ask how they use Zoho today.
2. Ask where property lead follow-up, agent assignment, or reporting fails.
3. Ask which automations they do not trust.
4. Ask how bookings, payments, commissions, or documents are tracked.
5. Explain the audit.
6. Offer fixed price and timeline.
7. Ask for access requirements and start date.

End-of-week target:

- 50 total messages sent
- 10-15 replies
- 3-5 calls
- 1-2 paid audits

## Week 4: Deliver And Convert

For each audit:

- Deliver the report.
- Walk through the findings on a call.
- Show the top 3 fixes.
- Offer the implementation sprint.

Implementation pitch:

> The audit shows the highest-value fixes are lead capture, agent assignment, pipeline cleanup, workflow rebuild, and booking/payment/commission handoff. I can implement these in a focused 2-4 week sprint so the team has a cleaner lead-to-closing process.

End-of-month target:

- 3 paid audits
- 1 implementation sprint
- 3 new proof assets
- 20 LinkedIn posts
- 50-80 relevant messages

## Weekly Metrics

Track:

- Profile views
- Relevant inbound messages
- Outreach sent
- Replies
- Calls booked
- Audits sold
- Implementation proposals sent
- Implementation projects closed

## Rules

- Do not sell hourly work first.
- Do not pitch every Zoho capability you know.
- Do not chase tiny vague tasks.
- Do not build a SaaS before selling audits.
- Do not wait for a perfect website.
- Do not post generic motivation content.
- Do not talk to every business type at once. Stay focused on real estate.

## What To Say When Someone Asks For Hourly Rate

> For open-ended implementation, I can work hourly after scope is clear. But for messy real estate Zoho setups, I usually recommend starting with a fixed audit first. It gives us the exact issue list, priority order, and implementation scope, so you are not paying hourly for guesswork.

## What To Say When Someone Says "Can You Just Fix One Workflow?"

> Yes, I can fix a specific workflow. Before changing it, I would want to check what triggers it, what records it touches, and whether any other automations depend on it. Zoho issues often look isolated but are connected underneath, especially when leads, agents, listings, bookings, payments, and reports share the same data.
